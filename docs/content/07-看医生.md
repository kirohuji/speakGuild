# 任务链 07 · 看医生 🏥

> **场景**：🏥 搞定麻烦 | **难度**：L2~L4 (IELTS 6~8) | **任务**：3 | **主题**：健康/症状/医疗

---

## 剧情概述

在英语国家看病是很多非母语者最害怕的场景——症状描述不准确可能导致误诊，医疗术语像天书，药价还让人肉疼。这条链训练你在医疗场景下清晰、准确、有底气地表达。

---

## 任务 1 · 电话预约，说不出自己的症状

**情境**：你咳嗽了两周不见好，决定预约看医生。电话那头的接线员问了一串问题，你需要在不查词典的情况下描述症状。

**NPC**：诊所接线员 — 按流程问问题，语速中等，但会用一些你不太熟的医学术语。

### 目标句块

| # | 句块 | 用途 |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | `I'd like to schedule an appointment—I've had a persistent cough for about two weeks now.` | 简明描述主诉 |
| 2 | `It's not severe, but it's not letting up either—that's why I thought I'd get it checked out.` | 说明严重程度和动机 |
| 3 | `I wouldn't say it's urgent, but sooner rather than later would be ideal.` | 表达时间偏好 |
| 4 | `Could you walk me through what I should bring—insurance card, ID, that sort of thing?` | 确认需要带的材料 |

### 对话流程（4 轮）

| 轮次 | NPC | 期望用户表达（参考） | 提示 |
|------|-----|-------------------|------|
| 1 | "Dr. Patel's office, how can I help?" | "Hi, I'd like to schedule an appointment. I've had a persistent cough for about two weeks—it's not letting up." | `persistent` / `not letting up` |
| 2 | "Is it a dry cough or a productive cough—are you bringing anything up?" | "Mostly dry, though occasionally there's some phlegm. No fever, no shortness of breath—it's just lingering." | `productive cough` / `phlegm` / `lingering` |
| 3 | "We have an opening tomorrow at 10:30, or Friday at 2 p.m." | "Tomorrow at 10:30 would be ideal. It's not urgent per se, but sooner rather than later, you know?" | `not urgent per se` / `sooner rather than later` |
| 4 | "You're booked. Bring your insurance card and a photo ID." | "Got it—insurance card and ID. Thanks for squeezing me in." | `squeezing me in` |

## 任务 2 · 医生问病史，有些词你真不会

**情境**：医生问你的病史和过敏史。有些词你隐约知道意思但不确定，有些完全陌生。你需要在不乱说的前提下尽量提供准确信息。

**NPC**：Dr. Patel — 耐心但高效，如果发现你听不懂会换种说法，但不会主动降低专业水平。

### 目标句块

| # | 句块 | 用途 |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | `Could you rephrase that? I'm not entirely sure I caught the medical term.` | 请对方换种说法 |
| 2 | `I don't have any pre-existing conditions that I'm aware of, but I haven't had a comprehensive check-up in a while.` | 诚实但有保留地回答 |
| 3 | `When you say ___, do you mean ___ or something else?` | 用自己的理解确认术语 |
| 4 | `To the best of my knowledge, I don't have any allergies to medication.` | 谨慎陈述（留余地） |

### 对话流程（4 轮）

| 轮次 | NPC | 期望用户表达（参考） | 提示 |
|------|-----|-------------------|------|
| 1 | "Any pre-existing conditions I should know about? Diabetes, hypertension, asthma—anything along those lines?" | "Not that I'm aware of—though I haven't had a comprehensive check-up in a while. To the best of my knowledge, I'm in decent health aside from this cough." | `pre-existing` / `to the best of my knowledge` |
| 2 | "And are you on any regular medication? Prescription or over-the-counter?" | "No regular medication. Occasionally I'll take ibuprofen for headaches, but nothing on a daily basis." | `prescription` / `over-the-counter` / `on a daily basis` |
| 3 | "Any known allergies? Particularly to antibiotics like penicillin?" | "Could you rephrase the antibiotic part? I'm not entirely sure I caught the term. When you say penicillin—is that a specific type of antibiotic?" | `rephrase` / `caught the term` |
| 4 | "Yes, it's a common one. Some people get a rash or worse. Any reactions to medications in the past?" | "To the best of my knowledge, no. I've taken antibiotics before without any adverse reactions." | `adverse reactions` |

## 任务 3 · 开的药太贵，问有没有便宜替代

**情境**：医生给你开了一种止咳药，你去药房取药时发现保险不覆盖，自费要 $85。你想知道有没有更便宜但同样有效的替代药。

**NPC**：药剂师 — 专业、乐于帮忙，但不能主动建议换药（需要你开口问）。

### 目标句块

| # | 句块 | 用途 |
|---|------|------|
| 1 | `I've just had this prescription filled, and the price caught me off guard—it's $85 out of pocket.` | 说明价格让你意外 |
| 2 | `Is there a generic equivalent that might be more affordable?` | 询问仿制药替代 |
| 3 | `I'm not questioning the doctor's judgment—I'm just trying to manage the cost.` | 澄清不是在质疑医生 |
| 4 | `If there's a cheaper alternative with comparable efficacy, I'd be open to that.` | 表达接受替代方案 |

### 对话流程（3 轮）

| 轮次 | NPC | 期望用户表达（参考） | 提示 |
|------|-----|-------------------|------|
| 1 | "Here's your prescription. That'll be $85—your insurance doesn't cover this particular brand." | "Oh—that caught me off guard. $85 out of pocket is a bit steep. Is there a generic equivalent that might be more affordable?" | `caught me off guard` / `generic equivalent` |
| 2 | "There is a generic version. I'd need to call the doctor to get the prescription changed. It'd be about $15 instead." | "That would make a significant difference. I'm not questioning the doctor's judgment—I'm just trying to manage the cost. If you could make that call, I'd really appreciate it." | `make a significant difference` / `manage the cost` |
| 3 | "Sure thing. Give me five minutes—the doctor usually approves these quickly." | "Take your time. I'm just grateful there's an option with comparable efficacy that doesn't break the bank." | `comparable efficacy` / `break the bank` |
